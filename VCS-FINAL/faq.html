Frequently Asked Questions

As always, feel free to email or call anytime with your questions. The questions below are some of the most common.

A stone pad is the best look but site prep is not needed if the spot for the shed is within 6 or 8″ of level on solid ground. The larger sheds usually need crushed stone in order to level the site. Stone is also a good idea in wet areas. Crushed stone should be within 1" to 2″ of level before I arrive to start the shed.

I should have access for trailer and materials within 25′ of the shed site. I need access to electricity within 100′. If no electricity is available, there will be a minimal charge for using a generator.

Every town can have different rules and regulations. Any permitting or zoning issues are the responsibility of the customer. That info usually can be found at your local town hall.

The prices for a Utility, Select or Custom Shed are on the website. That covers a complete shed with locking double doors. If you would like to add custom features that aren’t included with your shed go to the options and upgrades page. Those prices are listed there. No sales tax if I build your shed. I’m providing a construction service by building your shed on your site. Why pay an extra 7% in sales tax for a drop-off-the-back-of-a-truck shed?

The listed prices are good within 70 miles of Rutland, Vermont. I often travel farther. Contact me to see if travel charges would apply.

You can pay for your shed with cash, credit card, or check.
